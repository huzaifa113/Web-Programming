Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Muhammad Ali Jinnah (1876–1948) was a prominent political leader, statesman, and the founding father of Pakistan. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, then part of British India, Jinnah emerged as a key figure in the Indian independence movement and later played a crucial role in the creation of Pakistan in 1947.

Allama M Iqbal

Allama Iqbal (1877–1938), also known as Muhammad Iqbal, was a renowned philosopher, poet, and politician in British India who is widely regarded as one of the most important figures in Urdu literature. Born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot (now in Pakistan), Iqbal's works have had a profound impact on the cultural, social, and political landscape of the Indian subcontinent
